Basic Equipment Catalog

A helmet is an essential equipment for caving. It protects the caver's head from impacts and small falling rocks.

The jumpsuit helps the caver maintain body temperature more easily inside the cave, protects the body against infection risks, and includes extra protective parts on areas such as knees and elbows, preventing injuries while moving in the cave.

With boots, the caver can walk comfortably on wet cave surfaces without slipping, and the feet are protected from impacts. Choosing high boots is important when crossing shallow waters to prevent water from entering the boots.

Gloves ensure that the caver does not directly touch the cave surface with their hands, reducing the risk of infection. Gloves made of non-slip materials like rubber provide extra grip inside the cave.

The belt is the fundamental climbing equipment used by cavers when entering vertical caves. Other technical equipment is attached to it.

The chest harness is the upper part of the belt. It connects with the harness through shoulder ropes and croll.

The chest ascender (croll) is a climbing equipment that works on the principle of a jumar. It moves only upward on the rope.

The hand ascender, like the croll, is a climbing equipment that moves only in one direction on the rope. It is held in the hand and, by pressing the attached foot loop, allows ascending on the rope.

The descender is a descending equipment. It is attached to the harness with a carabiner. Thanks to its dual pulley structure, it enables controlled descent on the rope.

The stop descender, unlike a normal descender, includes a stop lever. It locks itself as long as the lever is not pressed.

Delta is the basic equipment attached to the harness. All other technical equipment (chest ascender, hand ascender, long and short cowstails, descender, etc.) is connected to the delta.

Karabiner is the most common equipment used in caving and other climbing sports. It is used to connect equipment during rope work, such as opening rope lines, connecting equipment at stations, and climbing.

Long-short is a safety device consisting of a long and a short rope and asymmetric carabiners at the ends. It is used to connect to stations in the cave.

The foot loop is an equipment that allows the caver to ascend by using foot power while connected to the hand ascender. The caver ascends by stepping on the foot loop during the ascent.

Static rope is a type of rope used in caving. It is used for opening rope lines in the cave. The length of the ropes is selected based on the cave structure, and it has minimal elasticity.

Dynamic rope is used in caving to connect the caver's equipment. It is both a long and short dynamic rope. It has more elasticity compared to static rope.

Tamtam is a technical equipment used in caving to test and prepare the cave surface before drilling. It allows checking and smoothing the surface without creating holes.

Drill is used in caving to drill the prepared surface using tamtam. It is an essential tool for creating anchor points and stations in the cave.

Bolt is inserted into the hole drilled with a drill. It is hammered into the wall with tamtam. Afterward, an ear is attached to its end, completing the station.

Hurc is the bag where cavers carry their belongings (food, first aid kit, etc.) while exploring the cave. It is resistant to impact, abrasion, and water.
